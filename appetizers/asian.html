How to Make Spring Rolls

Ingredients (Makes about 10-12 spring rolls):

  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 1/2 cup julienned bell peppers (any color)
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts (optional)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped spring onions
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil

For the wrappers:

10-12 spring roll wrappers (store-bought or homemade)

For sealing:

tablespoon all-purpose flour mixed with 2 tablespoons water (to make a paste)

For frying:

Vegetable oil for deep frying

Prepare the spring Rolls Filling:

  • Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add the minced garlic and sautÃ© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Add the shredded cabbage, grated carrots, bell peppers, and bean sprouts. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  • Add the soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Mix well and cook for another minute.
  • Remove the filling from the heat and let it cool completely.

Assemble the Spring Rolls:

  • Place a spring roll wrapper on a flat surface with one corner pointing toward you (like a diamond).
  • Add about 2 tablespoons of the cooled filling near the corner closest to you.
  • Fold the corner over the filling, tucking it in tightly.
  • Fold in the left and right sides of the wrapper to form an envelope.
  • Roll the wrapper tightly toward the top corner.
  • Seal the edge with the flour paste to keep the roll intact.
  • Repeat with the remaining wrappers and filling.

Fry the Spring Rolls:

  • Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  • Once the oil is hot (about 350Â°F or 175Â°C), gently slide in a few spring rolls, being careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
  • Remove and place on paper towels to drain excess oil.

Serve:

  • Serve the spring rolls hot with dipping sauces like sweet chili sauce, soy sauce, or a spicy peanut sauce.

How to Make Dim Sum (Steamed Dumplings)

For the dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup hot water

For the filling:

Ingredients (Makes 12-15 dumplings)::

  • 1/2 cup ground pork (or chicken/shrimp)
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped cabbage
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped spring onions
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

Prepare the Dough:

  • In a mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and hot water. Mix until a dough forms.
  • Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

Make the Filling:

  • In a bowl, combine the ground pork, cabbage, spring onions, ginger, garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Mix well until all the ingredients are combined.

Shape the Dumplings:

  • Divide the dough into small pieces (about the size of a golf ball). Roll each piece into a thin circle.
  • Place 1 teaspoon of filling in the center of each circle.
  • Fold the wrapper in half over the filling and pleat the edges to seal tightly.

Steam the Dumplings:

  • Line a bamboo or metal steamer with parchment paper or cabbage leaves to prevent sticking.
  • the dumplings in the steamer, leaving space between them.
  • Steam over boiling water for 8-10 minutes until the filling is fully cooked.

Serve:

  • Serve the dim sum hot with dipping sauces like soy sauce, chili oil, or vinegar.
  • Enjoy your delicate and delicious dumplings! ðŸŒ¸

How to Make Sushi Rolls (Maki Sushi)

For the Sushi rise:

Ingredients (Makes 4 rolls):::

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

For the Sushi Rolls:

  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 1/2 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, julienned
  • 4 imitation crab sticks, halved lengthwise
  • Wasabi, soy sauce, and pickled ginger for serving

For serving:

  • Wasabi
  • soy sauce
  • ginger

Prepare the Sushi Rice:

  • Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  • Cook the rice with 2 1/2 cups of water in a rice cooker or pot.
  • In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved.
  • Once the rice is cooked, gently mix in the vinegar mixture. Let it cool to room temperature.

Prepare the Rolling Station:

  • Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface and cover it with plastic wrap.
  • Place a sheet of nori shiny-side down on the bamboo mat.

Assemble the Sushi Roll:

  • Spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top uncovered.
  • Place your desired fillings (fish, cucumber, avocado, etc.) horizontally across the center of the rice.
  • Roll the bamboo mat tightly, starting from the bottom edge, to form a cylinder. Wet the uncovered edge with water to seal the roll.

Cut the Rolls:

  • Use a sharp knife to cut the roll into 6-8 equal pieces.
  • Clean the knife with water between cuts for clean slices.

Serve:

  • Arrange the sushi rolls on a plate and serve with soy sauce, pickled ginger, and wasabi.
